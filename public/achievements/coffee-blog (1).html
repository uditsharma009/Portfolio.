Section 01

Types of Coffee: Know Your Drinks

From the punchy solo espresso to the silky cold brew, here is your no-jargon guide to the most popular coffee styles — ordered from boldest to mildest.

⚡

Espresso

The foundation of almost every café drink. A single ~30ml shot of concentrated coffee, intensely flavoured and quick to drink.

Very Bold
🫧

Americano

Espresso diluted with hot water. Full coffee flavour with less intensity — a great choice for those moving from instant coffee.

Bold
🥛

Latte

Espresso plus steamed milk with a light foam top. Creamy and mild — the most popular café order globally. Perfect for beginners.

Mild
☁️

Cappuccino

Equal thirds: espresso, steamed milk, and thick foam. Stronger than a latte, lighter than a straight espresso.

Medium
🧊

Cold Brew

Ground coffee steeped in cold water for 12–24 hours. Smooth, naturally sweet, and low in acidity. Very different from iced coffee.

Medium-Bold
🌊

Pour-Over

Hot water poured slowly over grounds in a filter. Clean, nuanced, floral notes. The enthusiast's favourite home method.

Medium
🔩

French Press

Full immersion brewing — bold, rich, slightly heavier body. The easiest and most forgiving entry point for home brewing.

Rich & Bold
🪶

Flat White

Like a latte, but smaller and with a higher espresso-to-milk ratio. More intense flavour — recommended for beginner espresso lovers.

Medium-Bold

Section 03

Health Benefits of Coffee

Science has had a lot of good things to say about your daily cup. Here are the evidence-backed benefits of moderate coffee consumption.

🫐

Rich in Antioxidants

For many people, coffee is the single largest source of antioxidants in their diet — surpassing fruits and vegetables, per Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health.

🧠

Sharper Focus

Caffeine blocks adenosine, a sleep-promoting neurotransmitter, improving alertness and reaction time within 30–45 minutes of your first sip.

🔥

Metabolic Boost

Studies link moderate intake (2–4 cups/day) to a 3–11% increase in metabolic rate, making it one of the few natural substances with this effect.

😊

Mood Support

A Harvard study found that drinking 2–3 cups per day was associated with a 15% reduced risk of depression in regular coffee drinkers.

🫀

Liver Health

Regular coffee consumption is associated with a significantly lower risk of liver cirrhosis and liver disease — one of coffee's most researched benefits.

⚖️

Longevity Links

Multiple large-scale studies associate regular moderate coffee consumption with a modestly lower risk of premature death from various causes.

⚠️ A note on moderation: The optimal range is 2–4 cups per day. Exceeding 400mg of caffeine daily may cause anxiety, disrupted sleep, or an elevated heart rate in some individuals. Listen to your body.

Section 04

Beginner's Quick-Start Guide

New to coffee? Don't overthink it. Three simple steps to find your ideal cup — no barista training required.

1

Start with a Latte or Flat White

The milk softens espresso's bitterness and eases you in gently. Most baristas actually recommend a flat white for beginners — it has a better espresso-to-milk balance than a latte.

2

Brew at Home with a French Press

Pick up a French press (available under ₹800) and a bag of medium-roast ground coffee. It is the most forgiving method and produces a genuinely great cup every time.

3

Experiment and Personalise

Try different milk types (oat milk pairs especially well for a subtle natural sweetness) and roast levels — light for fruity notes, dark for bold chocolatey bitterness.

💡

Reddit & Community Tip

The most upvoted beginner question on r/Coffee is: "What's the easiest brewing method to start with?" — The consistent top answer: French press. Low cost, no filters, forgiving grind size, and bold results.

FAQs

Your Coffee Questions, Answered

What are the most popular types of coffee?
The most popular globally are espresso, latte, cappuccino, Americano, and cold brew. Espresso serves as the base for most café drinks, while cold brew has seen over 300% growth in demand in the past decade. For beginners, a latte is typically the easiest starting point.
What is the difference between cold brew and iced coffee?
Cold brew is made by steeping coarse grounds in cold water for 12–24 hours, resulting in a smooth, naturally sweet, low-acid drink. Iced coffee is simply hot-brewed coffee poured over ice, which tends to be more bitter and diluted. They are very different despite looking similar.
Which brewing method makes the best cup?
"Best" depends on your taste. Pour-over produces the cleanest, most nuanced flavour. French press gives a bold, full-bodied cup. Espresso machines deliver the most café-authentic result. For beginners at home, French press is the most forgiving and cost-effective starting point.
Is coffee good for your health?
Moderate consumption (2–4 cups/day) is associated with improved focus, metabolic support, and a reduced risk of certain liver conditions. Coffee is also one of the richest dietary sources of antioxidants. However, exceeding 400mg of caffeine daily may cause negative effects for some people.
What is the best coffee for someone who doesn't like bitterness?
Opt for light-to-medium roast beans, which have less bitterness than dark roasts. Milk-based drinks like lattes or flat whites soften espresso's sharp edge. Cold brew is naturally sweeter and less bitter due to its cold extraction process. Oat milk also pairs especially well for a creamy, subtle sweetness.
How do I choose the right coffee roast?
Light roasts have higher acidity and preserve fruity or floral flavours. Medium roasts balance acidity and body — ideal for most beginners. Dark roasts are bold, bitter, and low-acid with a smoky or chocolatey finish. Start with medium roast if you are unsure.
